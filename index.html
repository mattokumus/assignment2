üìñ About This Project
"Does the European Court of Human Rights Treat Countries Differently?"

A comprehensive statistical analysis of 1,904 substantive ECtHR cases (1968-2020) examining systematic country differences in violation findings using multi-method triangulation.

üî¨ Multi-Method Approach

  • Hypothesis Testing: Chi-square tests, proportion tests, and effect size analysis comparing Eastern vs Western Europe (p < 0.001)
  • Logistic Regression: Country-level models with L1 regularization controlling for article type, year, and applicant type (Pseudo R¬≤ = 0.226, AUC = 0.801)

  • Machine Learning: Random Forest, XGBoost, and Gradient Boosting with temporal validation (2000-2014 train, 2015-2020 test)
‚ö†Ô∏è Important Caveats
Statistical significance ‚â† Discrimination. These findings do NOT necessarily indicate judicial bias. Systematic differences may reflect legitimate factors including:
  • Genuine human rights conditions and rule-of-law variations across countries
  • Case complexity, quality of legal representation, and strength of evidence (not captured in data)
  • Selection bias: only cases surviving domestic remedies and admissibility thresholds reach ECtHR
  • Post-communist transition challenges vs discriminatory scrutiny (observational data cannot distinguish)
This analysis establishes correlation, not causation. See full documentation for detailed methodological limitations.
